1080p HD — How It Works
Video Encoding
  • Encoded at 1920×1080 (Full HD) on the sender's device using H.264 or VP9
  • Hardware GPU encoding used where available (near-zero CPU impact)
  • Sent as the HIGH simulcast layer at ~2.5 Mbps upload
  • Forwarded at full 1080p to participants who are spotlighted or active speaker
  • Thumbnail tiles receive the 480p or 240p layer automatically
  • 60fps available with the Premium HD add-on (default is 30fps)
Where You See 1080p
  • Active speaker (Speaker View) — always 1080p if sender supports it
  • Spotlighted participant (host-pinned for all) — always 1080p
  • Your personal pinned participant — 1080p local view
  • Shared screen — up to 1080p at 5fps (standard) or 15fps (HD sharing)
  • Gallery View thumbnails — 480p per tile (1080p reserved for active speaker)
  • Recordings — 1080p MP4 for cloud recordings on paid plans
1080p is not always visible on small tiles
Even if a participant is sending 1080p, a small 200px gallery tile only needs 480p — sending more would waste bandwidth. FasaFas's simulcast engine sends 1080p only to the subscribers who actually need it, making the system efficient even at scale.
