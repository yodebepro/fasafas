Simulcast

Simulcast means sending three quality layers simultaneously — 1080p, 480p, and 240p. The SFU forwards whichever layer is right for each subscriber's network, without any re-encoding.

How Simulcast Works
  1. Your browser encodes 3 streams from one camera input
    WebRTC encodes your video at three resolutions simultaneously using different spatial and temporal scalability. All three share the same RTP session with different SSRCs.
  2. All three streams uploaded to the SFU
    Total upload: ~3.5 Mbps (1080p: 2.5 Mbps + 480p: 0.8 Mbps + 240p: 0.15 Mbps). The SFU receives all three on the same WebRtcTransport.
  3. SFU creates one Consumer per subscriber
    Each subscriber has their own Consumer, which is independently configured to receive any simulcast layer. Consumers for different subscribers can receive different layers from the same Producer.
  4. SFU selects layer per Consumer based on subscriber's BWE
    The SFU watches REMB/TWCC feedback from each subscriber and sets their Consumer's preferred layer accordingly. Switches happen in real-time as network conditions change.
VP9 SVC — The Simulcast Alternative
SVC vs Simulcast
  • Simulcast: 3 completely separate encodings (3× encode CPU on sender)
  • SVC: 1 encoding with embedded quality layers (more efficient for sender)
  • VP9 with SVC saves ~30% upload bandwidth vs simulcast
  • Both give the SFU the same flexibility to forward different qualities per subscriber
  • FasaFas uses SVC on Chrome/Edge (VP9 supported) automatically
  • Falls back to simulcast on Safari (H.264 only, no SVC)
Spatial vs Temporal Layers
  • Spatial layers: different resolutions (1080p → 480p → 240p)
  • Temporal layers: same resolution, different frame rates (30fps → 15fps → 7fps)
  • FasaFas uses both: spatial for resolution drops, temporal for frame rate drops
  • Temporal-only drops are imperceptible at first — video just looks slightly less smooth
  • Spatial drops are more noticeable but happen only with significant bandwidth loss
