Speaker View

Speaker View shows the current active speaker in a large main area with a row of thumbnails for other participants — so the focus is always on who's talking.

Speaker View — How It Works
Main + Thumbnail Layout
  • Active speaker fills ~75% of the screen at 1080p
  • A row of thumbnail tiles at the bottom or side shows other participants
  • Thumbnail strip is scrollable for large meetings
  • When speaker changes, main view transitions with a smooth 0.3s crossfade
  • Your own tile appears in the thumbnail strip (not in main) by default
  • Main view can be any shared screen when someone is screen sharing
Side-by-Side Mode
  • When someone shares their screen, main view splits into two panels
  • Left panel: shared screen content
  • Right panel: active speaker video
  • Draggable divider to resize each panel
  • Can revert to full-screen share with toggle
Speaker View Controls
  • Switch to Speaker View: View → Speaker (or keyboard Alt+S on Windows)
  • Swap positions: click the swap icon to put speaker video in thumbnail and screen share in main
  • Pin a participant: hover their thumbnail → Pin → they stay in main view regardless of who speaks
  • Resize thumbnail strip: drag the top edge of the strip up or down
  • Hide thumbnail strip: click the collapse arrow — speaker fills full screen
  • Reorder thumbnails: drag thumbnails to rearrange within the strip
  • Double-click any thumbnail → that participant goes to main view
Voice Activity Detection (VAD) — Speaker Switching
  • VAD runs on the SFU — analyzes audio energy levels from all participants in real time
  • The participant with the highest audio energy for >500ms becomes the "active speaker"
  • 500ms threshold prevents switching on brief interjections ("uh-huh", "yeah")
  • Noise suppression runs before VAD — keyboard sounds, background noise excluded
  • If background noise triggers false switches, increase your noise suppression level
  • Host can pin a participant to prevent VAD from switching away from them
  • Speaker history: the last 3 speakers' thumbnails are highlighted in the strip
