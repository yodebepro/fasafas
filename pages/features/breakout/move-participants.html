Move Participants

Move any participant from one room to another at any time — before rooms open or while they're actively running. Participants move instantly with no disruption to either room.

How to Move a Participant
  1. Open Breakout Rooms panel
    The panel shows all rooms with participant names listed inside each room.
  2. Find the participant to move
    Scroll to locate the participant in their current room, or use the search box at the top of the panel.
  3. Click their name → Move to
    A dropdown appears listing all other rooms. Click the destination room name.
  4. Confirm (optional)
    Depending on your settings, a confirmation prompt may appear. If rooms are closed, the participant is placed into the new room when they open.
Drag & Drop on Desktop
On the desktop app, you can drag a participant's name directly from one room column to another in the Breakout Rooms panel grid view. This is faster than the dropdown for moving multiple participants quickly.
What Happens During a Live Move
Participant Experience
  • A small notification appears: "The host has moved you to Group B"
  • A 3-second countdown: "Moving to Group B in 3... 2... 1..."
  • Their audio and video automatically reconnect to the new room
  • Any chat they were in stays in the original room (not carried over)
  • Their personal notes and My Notes continue uninterrupted
  • They do NOT have to click anything — the move is automatic
Effect on Rooms
  • Original room: a "Participant X has left" message shows in room chat
  • Destination room: a "Participant X has joined" message shows in room chat
  • Neither room's session is interrupted or paused
  • Recording (if active) captures the move as a participant join/leave event
  • Whiteboard states in both rooms are unaffected
Common Scenarios for Moving Participants
Rebalancing Groups
  • One room has 10 people, another has 3 — move 3–4 people to balance
  • A participant joined late — their room was auto-assigned wrong, move them
  • A participant didn't get the invite dialog in time — they're stuck in main, move them to their room
Skill or Role Matching
  • Two engineers ended up in different rooms — merge them for a technical discussion
  • Need a facilitator in a leaderless group — move a co-host there
  • A senior leader wants to observe a specific team — move them in
Conflict Resolution
  • Two participants are clashing — move one to a different room diplomatically
  • A participant is disrupting their room — move them to main session or a quiet room
  • A guest should only be in specific rooms — move them as agenda changes
