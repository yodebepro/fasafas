Broadcast Messages

Send a text message to every breakout room simultaneously. The message appears as a banner overlay inside each room — no one misses it. Perfect for time warnings, guidance updates, or summaries.

How to Broadcast a Message
  1. Open Breakout Rooms panel
    Only the host or co-host can send broadcasts. The panel must be open in the main session.
  2. Click "Broadcast a message to all"
    A text compose box appears at the bottom of the Breakout Rooms panel.
  3. Type your message
    Up to 500 characters. Plain text only — no images or files. Keep it concise so it fits on one line.
  4. Click "Broadcast"
    Message is delivered to all open rooms instantly. It appears as a banner at the top of every participant's screen for 10 seconds, then fades.
📢 Broadcast Technical Details
Delivery timeInstant — appears in all rooms simultaneously (<1 second)
Display locationOrange banner overlay at top of participant's screen
Display duration10 seconds, then fades out automatically
Max message length500 characters
Who can broadcastHost and co-hosts only
Notification soundSubtle chime plays when banner appears
Message Examples by Scenario
Time & Pacing
  • "⏱ 5 minutes remaining — please start wrapping up your discussion."
  • "Great energy! You have 10 more minutes. Start deciding on your top 3 ideas."
  • "2 minutes left — please nominate one person to share your group's insights."
  • "Time is up! Great work everyone. Returning to the main session shortly."
Guidance & Instructions
  • "Remember to use the whiteboard to map your ideas — it will be shared at the end."
  • "Please choose a spokesperson before rooms close. They'll present first."
  • "Revised prompt: focus on the *customer* perspective, not the internal process."
  • "A clarification on question 3: 'budget' means total annual, not per-quarter."
Best Practices for Broadcasting
  • Keep messages short — under 80 characters if possible. Longer messages may truncate on small screens.
  • Lead with the most important word: "⏱ 5 MINUTES LEFT" not "Just a reminder that you have 5 minutes left"
  • Don't broadcast more than once every 2–3 minutes — too many interruptions break concentration
  • Use for guidance changes, time warnings, and encouragement — not casual chat
  • Pair a broadcast with joining a specific room if that room needs individual attention
  • After the session: note what you broadcast so you can include it in the post-meeting summary
