AI Avatar (Realistic)

A photorealistic 3D avatar built from a brief 30-second video capture. It mirrors your facial expressions, head movement, and lip sync in real time — so you stay present without being on camera.

Setting Up Your Realistic Avatar
  1. Go to Settings → Video → AI Avatar
    Click "Create Realistic Avatar". This is a one-time setup process.
  2. Record a 30-second face scan
    Follow the on-screen prompts: look straight, then left, right, up, down. Ensure good lighting. The scan is processed locally on your device (no video uploaded).
  3. AI builds your avatar model (1–2 minutes)
    A lightweight 3D facial mesh is created using your face geometry. The model is stored encrypted in your account.
  4. Authenticate with liveness check
    Before using your avatar in any meeting, a liveness check confirms you are the registered person — preventing misuse by others with access to your device.
  5. Activate in a meeting
    In the meeting join preview or during a meeting: click caret next to Stop Video → Choose Virtual Camera → AI Avatar (Realistic).
Technology Behind the Realistic Avatar
Face Tracking
  • 52 facial action unit detection (based on Apple ARKit model)
  • Head pose estimation: pitch, yaw, roll angles
  • Eye blink and gaze direction tracking
  • Lip shape tracking (viseme mapping for lip sync)
  • Emotion blendshapes: smile, frown, surprise, etc.
  • Runs at 30fps on CPU / 60fps with GPU acceleration
Rendering
  • 3D mesh rendered via WebGL in the browser
  • PBR (Physically Based Rendering) for realistic skin texture
  • Dynamic lighting follows your real background lighting conditions
  • Hair and eyebrows rendered separately for realism
  • Anti-aliasing applied before frame export to WebRTC
  • Requires: NVIDIA GTX 1060+ or Apple M1+ or equivalent
When to Use Your Realistic Avatar
Good Use Cases
  • Days when you're not camera-ready (bad hair, no makeup, illness)
  • Traveling — unpredictable or poor backgrounds
  • Long back-to-back meetings where camera fatigue sets in
  • Recording async Clips without needing to look at a camera
  • Presenting in large webinars where expression presence matters
Limitations
  • Glasses tracking can occasionally slip
  • Extreme head rotations (>45°) may lose tracking momentarily
  • Very dark rooms can confuse the face detection model
  • Not suitable for video calls requiring recording for legal purposes
  • Visible latency of ~80–100ms between real expression and avatar
