TURN Relay Servers

TURN servers relay media when a direct WebRTC connection is impossible — typically behind strict corporate firewalls, symmetric NAT, or networks that block UDP.

What TURN Does and When It's Needed
  • ~20–30% of enterprise users need TURN — their firewall or NAT prevents direct connections
  • TURN = Traversal Using Relays around NAT (RFC 5766)
  • Instead of connecting directly, you connect to the TURN server, which relays your media to the SFU
  • ICE tests direct paths first (lowest latency) and only falls back to TURN if direct fails
  • TURN over TCP port 443: works through the strictest firewalls that only allow HTTPS traffic
  • Adds 20–40ms latency compared to a direct connection (relay adds one extra network hop)
  • TURN handles all media — audio, video, data channels — not just signaling
TURN Server Authentication
  • TURN requires authentication to prevent abuse (anyone can't just relay through your TURN)
  • FasaFas uses HMAC-SHA1 time-limited credentials (RFC 8489)
  • When your ICE agent requests TURN credentials, signal-service generates a temporary TURN username + password
  • Username format: timestamp:userId
  • Password: HMAC-SHA1(username, turnSharedSecret)
  • TTL: 24 hours — credentials expire after the meeting session
  • TURN server validates credentials on each allocation request
  • No TURN credential reuse across meetings — new credentials per session
