Deepfake Detection

FasaFas analyzes every participant's video frame-by-frame for artifacts of AI-generated media, alerting the host with a banner if synthetic video is detected — without storing any biometric data.

How Deepfake Detection Works
  • Temporal artifacts: Real faces have consistent micro-movements between frames. Deepfakes introduce subtle eyebrow, blink, and skin texture inconsistencies.
  • Frequency domain: GAN-generated faces leave patterns in the DCT frequency domain — invisible to humans, detectable algorithmically.
  • Lighting consistency: Real faces reflect light uniformly. Composited faces show lighting direction mismatches at edges.
  • Landmark tracking: 68 facial landmarks tracked across frames. Deepfakes show unnatural landmark drift.
  • Analysis runs at 2fps (every 500ms) — lightweight enough to run server-side without impacting meeting performance.
  • Model: fine-tuned EfficientNet-B4 on FaceForensics++ and DFDC datasets.
Alert Flow
  1. Detection confidence exceeds 85% for 3+ consecutive frames
    Score must be sustained — prevents false positives from single-frame compression artifacts
  2. Host receives a red banner (host only)
    '⚠️ Potential synthetic video detected for [Participant Name]. Review and take action.' — flagged participant is not notified
  3. Host can take action
    Options: Dismiss (false positive), Remove participant, Lock meeting, or Request liveness check (wave challenge)
  4. Audit log entry created
    All detections and host actions logged for compliance and legal review
Known Limitations
  • Not 100% accurate — commercial deepfakes designed to evade detection may score below threshold
  • Poor lighting, low-resolution cameras, or heavy compression can increase false positives
  • FasaFas AI Avatar (Realistic) is whitelisted — won't trigger the detector
  • Third-party virtual cameras (OBS, Snap Camera) may be flagged — hosts should use context to judge
  • Supplementary tool only — does not replace organizational security policies
  • Privacy: no video frames stored. Analysis is in-memory only. No biometric data retained.
