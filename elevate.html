How E911 Works in FasaFas
  • E911 (Enhanced 911) provides your registered address to the emergency dispatcher when you call 911
  • Standard internet calls don't automatically transmit location — E911 adds this capability
  • FasaFas transmits your registered E911 address to the PSAP (Public Safety Answering Point) via an ALI/ANI database lookup
  • Your phone number is also transmitted (ANI) so dispatchers can call you back
  • Nomadic E911: you can update your address from anywhere — FasaFas sends the current registered address at call time
Registering Your E911 Location
  1. Profile → Emergency Address
  2. Enter your full physical address — street, city, state, zip, country
    Must be a real, dispatchable address — PO boxes not accepted
  3. Verify the address
    FasaFas validates it against MSAG (Master Street Address Guide) for dispatchability
  4. Save. Address registered with your DID number.
  5. Update whenever you change work location — remote work, travel, co-working space
Always Update When You Move
E911 uses your REGISTERED address, not your actual GPS location. If you're working from a hotel and haven't updated your address, emergency services will be sent to your registered office address. Always update before calling 911 from a new location.
E911 Compliance
  • Ray Baum's Act (US): requires nomadic E911 capability for all enterprise VoIP users — FasaFas is compliant
  • Kari's Law: requires direct 911 dialing without dialing a prefix (no 9-911) — FasaFas compliant
  • Admin can require E911 registration before a user can make any phone calls
  • Admin Panel → Phone → E911 → view all user addresses and compliance status
  • Regular reminder emails sent to users who haven't updated their address in 90 days
