Named Cursors per Participant

Every collaborator's cursor is visible on the canvas in a unique color with their name attached — real-time presence awareness so you always know who's looking at what.

Named Cursors
  • Every logged-in user editing the whiteboard has a named cursor
  • Color: automatically assigned from a 12-color palette — unique per person per session
  • Label: your display name appears next to the cursor tip
  • Cursor movement synced at ~30fps — smooth real-time tracking
  • Cursor visible to all other participants in real time
  • Your own cursor: you see it normally — no duplicate label for yourself
  • Anonymous viewers: cursor shown with 'Guest' label if they haven't signed in
Presence Panel
  • Top-right of the whiteboard: avatar stack showing who's online
  • Click any avatar → camera jumps to their current viewport
  • Names shown on hover
  • Viewers vs editors: different badge style for read-only viewers
  • Guest count: shows '+ 3 guests' if anonymous viewers are present
  • Notification: brief toast when someone joins or leaves 'Ana joined the whiteboard'
Follow a Collaborator's Cursor
  • Click an avatar in the presence panel → 'Follow [Name]' option
  • Your viewport syncs to their viewport — you see exactly what they see
  • Canvas pans and zooms to match their view automatically
  • Exit follow mode: click 'Stop Following' or press Escape
  • Follow mode in presentations: host enables Follow Mode for everyone → all participants' views lock to presenter
Presenter Mode vs Follow Mode
Follow Mode: you choose to follow someone. Presenter Mode: the host forces all participants to follow their view — used for structured walkthroughs and demos.
