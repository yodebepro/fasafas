HLS Streaming & Playback

HLS (HTTP Live Streaming) lets you watch recordings instantly via browser without downloading — with adaptive bitrate, chapter navigation, speed control, and transcript sync.

HLS Technology — How Instant Streaming Works
  • HLS (RFC 8216) splits the recording into 6-second .ts (MPEG Transport Stream) segments
  • An m3u8 playlist file indexes all segments and their URLs
  • The browser downloads segments sequentially — starts playing as soon as the first few arrive
  • Segments are available as soon as the SFU generates them during the meeting — not waiting for MP4 mix
  • Multiple quality playlists: 1080p, 720p, 480p, 240p — browser picks based on download speed
  • CDN (CloudFront) caches segments globally — faster loading for distributed teams
  • Presigned segment URLs: expire after 24 hours, regenerated on demand — prevents unauthorized access

Adaptive Bitrate in Playback
  • ABR (Adaptive Bitrate) adjusts which HLS quality playlist is downloaded based on your download speed
  • If your connection is fast: 1080p segments downloaded — crystal clear playback
  • If your connection slows: player automatically switches to 720p or 480p — no buffering, just a quality drop
  • Buffer target: 3 segments ahead (18 seconds) — enough to handle brief network interruptions
  • Quality indicator: the gear icon in the player shows current quality level
  • Manual override: select a fixed quality in the quality menu to prevent ABR from switching
  • Mobile: default quality capped at 480p to preserve cellular data (configurable per device)
