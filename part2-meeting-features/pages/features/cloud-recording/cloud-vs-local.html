Cloud vs Local Recording

Cloud recording stores your meeting in FasaFas's secure infrastructure with AI post-processing. Local recording saves an MP4 directly to your device — no cloud, no AI, works on all plans.

Cloud Recording Details
  • Recorded by Mediasoup SFU server-side — zero CPU impact on your device
  • Available to stream within ~2 minutes of meeting end (HLS stream ready before MP4 mix is complete)
  • MP4 download typically ready 5–10 minutes after meeting end
  • Stored encrypted in Amazon S3 with per-account AES-256 key
  • AI pipeline (transcription + summary + chapters + action items) runs automatically after mixing
  • Accessible from any device after meeting via Hub → Recordings
  • Storage counted: ~0.8 GB per hour of meeting at 1080p Active Speaker layout
Local Recording Details
  • Recorded by your browser/app using MediaRecorder API — no server involvement
  • Saved to your system's Downloads folder as MP4 (or configured path)
  • Available immediately when you stop recording (no processing needed)
  • Quality depends on your device CPU — powerful machines produce higher quality local recordings
  • No AI post-processing — no automatic transcript, summary, or action items
  • Host can restrict local recording: participants cannot record unless host permits
  • Works in E2EE meetings — the only recording option when E2EE is enabled
  • File name format: [Meeting-Name]_[Date]_[Time].mp4
