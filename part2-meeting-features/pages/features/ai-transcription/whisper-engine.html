Whisper STT Engine

OpenAI Whisper large-v3 is the speech-to-text engine powering FasaFas transcription. It's one of the most accurate multilingual STT models available, handling accents, technical vocabulary, and noisy audio.

How Whisper Processes Meeting Audio
  1. Audio extracted from SFU
    The media-service subscribes to a mixed audio track from Mediasoup (all participants combined, or individual tracks for diarization). This audio is captured server-side — no extra permission needed from participants.
  2. Chunked into 3-second segments using VAD
    Voice Activity Detection identifies speech boundaries. Chunks are cut at silence points to avoid splitting words mid-utterance. Overlap of 0.5s between chunks prevents edge words being missed.
  3. Chunks sent to Whisper large-v3 API
    Each 3-second chunk encoded as 16kHz mono WAV and sent to the Whisper inference endpoint (either OpenAI API or self-hosted on FasaFas GPU infrastructure for Enterprise).
  4. Whisper returns text + word timestamps
    Response includes: transcribed text, word-level timestamps (start/end for every word), detected language, and confidence scores per segment.
  5. NLP post-processing
    A punctuation model adds commas, periods, and capitals. Custom vocabulary dictionary corrects product names, person names, and domain jargon.
Accuracy & Known Limitations
Accuracy (WER benchmarks)
  • English (clear audio, single speaker): ~2–4% WER (Word Error Rate)
  • English (multiple speakers, accents): ~5–10% WER
  • French, German, Spanish, Portuguese: ~5–8% WER
  • Japanese, Mandarin: ~8–12% WER (logographic languages)
  • Technical vocabulary: ~10–20% WER without custom dictionary
  • Custom dictionary: reduce technical WER to ~5–8%
Known Limitations
  • Heavy accents may increase WER by 5–15% vs native speech
  • Overlapping speech (two people talking at once) reduces accuracy significantly
  • Very loud background noise (construction, crowded cafe) raises WER
  • Proper nouns (company names, people's names) often need custom dictionary correction
  • Numbers and dates sometimes formatted inconsistently across chunks
  • Code-switching (mixing languages mid-sentence) may cause switching issues
Transcription in End-to-End Encrypted Meetings
E2EE Disables Server-Side Transcription
When End-to-End Encryption is enabled, the server cannot access the audio stream — so server-side transcription using Whisper is not possible. E2EE + transcription are mutually exclusive.
  • E2EE meetings: server-side transcription is disabled automatically
  • On-device alternative: NVIDIA Nemotron-Nano runs locally on compatible devices (no audio leaves device)
  • On-device transcription accuracy: lower than Whisper large-v3 (~15–20% WER)
  • On-device is available in the desktop app on Windows/Mac with Nemotron hardware requirements
  • Summary generation also disabled in E2EE mode (Claude cannot access the transcript)
