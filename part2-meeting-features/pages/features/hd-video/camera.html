Camera & Device Settings

The Camera Settings panel gives you full control over which camera is used, resolution, frame rate, hardware acceleration, and a live preview before and during meetings.

Camera Configuration Options
📷 Settings Panel — All Options
Camera deviceDropdown: lists all detected webcams + virtual cameras
ResolutionAuto / 720p / 1080p (depends on camera hardware capability)
Frame rateAuto / 15fps / 30fps / 60fps (Premium add-on for 60fps)
Mirror my videoToggle — mirrors your own self-view (default ON, others see un-mirrored)
HD enable/disableMaster toggle for HD upload (turn off to save bandwidth)
Hardware accelerationToggle — use GPU for encode/decode (recommended ON)
Aspect ratio16:9 (default) / 4:3 (older cameras)
Stop video on joinToggle — camera off by default when entering a meeting
Camera Test and Preview
  • Pre-meeting preview: camera shows in the join screen — adjust background, blur, effects before entering
  • During meeting: click the caret (∨) next to Stop Video → Video Settings → live preview updates as you change settings
  • Brightness check: a light level indicator warns if your room is too dark
  • Resolution indicator: shows the actual resolution being captured (vs requested)
  • Frame rate indicator: shows actual FPS delivered by the camera
  • Test speaker and microphone: same settings panel includes audio device test
Second Camera (Document Camera)
  • FasaFas supports sharing a second camera during a meeting
  • Useful for: document cameras, physical whiteboards, product demos, labs
  • Share screen → select "Second Camera" option
  • Your primary webcam continues to show your face in the video tile
  • Second camera appears as a screen share stream (not a separate video tile)
  • Supports any USB webcam, HDMI capture card, or virtual camera (OBS, Camo, etc.)
  • Resolution: up to 1080p at 15fps for second camera share
