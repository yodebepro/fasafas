Error Recovery

FasaFas has multiple layers of automatic error recovery — from brief network blips to full disconnections — designed to keep you in the meeting with minimal disruption.

Error Recovery Layers
  1. Layer 1 — NACK (Packet Retransmission)
    If a single RTP packet is lost, a NACK request is sent. The sender retransmits the missing packet. Handles <3% packet loss with no visible impact. Adds <30ms delay.
  2. Layer 2 — FEC (Forward Error Correction)
    Opus audio includes FEC — parity packets that let the receiver reconstruct up to one lost packet without retransmission. Best for bursty packet loss.
  3. Layer 3 — Simulcast Layer Drop
    If packet loss exceeds 5% or REMB drops significantly, SFU switches to a lower simulcast layer (1080p → 480p → 240p). Video continues — just at lower quality.
  4. Layer 4 — ICE Restart
    If the entire WebRTC transport fails (e.g., network interface change), ICE Restart re-negotiates the connection path. Audio may drop for 1–3 seconds. Session preserved.
  5. Layer 5 — Socket.io Reconnect + Full Re-Join
    If ICE Restart fails 3 times, Socket.io reconnects with exponential backoff (1s, 2s, 4s…). After socket reconnects, the meeting client re-joins the room automatically.
Recovery Timeline for a Network Drop
⏱️ What Happens When Your WiFi Drops for 5 Seconds
0–500msNACK retransmissions for missing packets. Jitter buffer masks loss. No visible impact.
500ms–2sPacket loss exceeds threshold. SFU drops to 240p layer for your stream. Audio switches to comfort noise.
2–5sICE consent checks fail. ICE Restart triggered. "Reconnecting..." indicator appears in meeting UI.
5–7sNetwork recovers. ICE Restart completes. New transport established (may route via TURN this time).
7–10sREMB recovers. SFU ramps back up to 480p, then 1080p. Audio full quality restored.
