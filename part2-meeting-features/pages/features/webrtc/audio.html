Audio Processing

FasaFas processes audio through a multi-stage pipeline — Opus encoding, echo cancellation, AI noise suppression, automatic gain control, and voice activity detection.

Opus Codec
🎵 Opus Audio Specs
CodecOpus (RFC 6716) — the WebRTC standard audio codec
Sample rate48 kHz (wideband voice) · 16 kHz (narrowband fallback)
ChannelsMono (voice calls) · Stereo (music/original sound mode)
Bitrate range6 kbps (very low) → 510 kbps (studio quality, original sound mode)
Default bitrate32 kbps (voice) — excellent quality at very low bandwidth
Latency20–120ms algorithmic delay (20ms frame size in FasaFas)
Why OpusBest codec for voice at low bitrate; supports FEC for packet loss recovery
Audio Processing Pipeline
  1. Capture (microphone → PCM)
    Raw 48kHz PCM audio captured from your microphone at 20ms frames (960 samples each).
  2. AEC — Acoustic Echo Cancellation
    Removes your speaker output from your mic input so others don't hear themselves echoed back. Uses a reference signal from your audio output and adaptive filtering (WebRTC AEC3).
  3. Noise Suppression (AI-powered)
    FasaFas's AI RNNoise model runs per 10ms frame, classifying each frequency band as speech or noise and suppressing non-speech frequencies. Removes keyboard typing, HVAC hum, background voices.
  4. AGC — Automatic Gain Control
    Normalizes your microphone volume so quiet speakers are boosted and loud speakers are reduced to a consistent level. Target: -18 dBFS.
  5. VAD — Voice Activity Detection
    Detects speech vs silence. During silence, DTX (Discontinuous Transmission) sends comfort noise instead of silent Opus packets — saving 70% bandwidth during quiet periods.
  6. Opus encoding → RTP
    20ms audio frame encoded to Opus bitstream, wrapped in RTP packet, encrypted with SRTP, sent to SFU.
