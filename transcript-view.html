Host Roaming

As host, you're not locked to one room. You can freely move between any breakout room and the main session at any time — supporting groups, observing discussions, or participating directly.

Host Tips
How Host Roaming Works
  1. Open Breakout Rooms panel
    From the main session, click Breakout Rooms. All active rooms are listed with live participant counts.
  2. Click "Join" next to a room
    You immediately move from the main session into that breakout room. Your video and audio switch to the room. Participants see you join just like any other participant.
  3. Participate or observe
    You can speak, share your screen, use the whiteboard, or simply listen. Your microphone is off by default when joining a room — you must unmute to speak.
  4. Leave the room
    Click Leave Breakout Room at the top of the screen. You return to the main session instantly. The room continues without you.
  5. Join a different room
    From the main session, click Join next to any other room. You can cycle through all rooms in any order, as many times as you want.
Does joining a room interrupt it?
Not significantly. Participants see "Host has joined the room" as a subtle notification. Your mic is muted by default so you don't accidentally interrupt. The room's recording (if any) continues uninterrupted.
Co-Host Room Management
Co-Host Abilities
  • Join and leave any breakout room (same as host)
  • Open the Breakout Rooms management panel
  • Move participants between rooms
  • Broadcast messages to all rooms
  • Extend the timer
  • Close all rooms early
  • Cannot create or delete rooms (host only)
Splitting Coverage
  • Assign one co-host per cluster of rooms for large sessions
  • E.g.: Host covers rooms 1–10, Co-host 1 covers 11–20, Co-host 2 covers 21–30
  • Use broadcast to coordinate with your co-hosts
  • Each co-host can respond to raise-hand requests independently
  • Only one person needs to be in each room at a time
Host Roaming Best Practices
  • Announce at the start: "I'll be dropping in on each group throughout the session" — sets expectations so participants aren't surprised
  • Spend 3–5 minutes per room for a 20-minute session — enough to check in without disrupting flow
  • Enter quietly (mic muted), observe briefly, then offer support only if needed
  • If a group is stuck, ask a guiding question rather than giving the answer
  • Use the Raise Hand alert list to prioritize which rooms need attention
  • Return to main session between rooms so you can see the broadcast panel and full participant list
  • For very large sessions (20+ rooms), use co-hosts to cover rooms simultaneously
